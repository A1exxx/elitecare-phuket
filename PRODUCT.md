# PRODUCT.md — Phuket Elite Care

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
A marketing site for **Phuket Elite Care Property Maintenance Co., Ltd.**, a licensed property-maintenance company on Phuket's west coast. The site's single job is to turn a villa owner's problem into a WhatsApp conversation with the office. There is no e-commerce, no login, no booking engine: the conversion event is a pre-filled WhatsApp message containing the details the office actually asks for (service, property type, bedrooms, bathrooms, size in m², location, preferred visit time).

## Users
Owners and long-stay renters of premium villas, townhouses and condos in Cherngtalay, Bang Tao, Laguna and the surrounding west-coast area. Two dominant profiles:

1. **Absentee owner.** Lives abroad, owns a villa used part of the year and rented out the rest. Cares about the property not degrading while unattended, and about having documentation (invoices, maintenance history) for a future sale. Communicates in English or Russian.
2. **Resident owner / long-stay tenant.** On the island now, with an immediate problem: the AC is failing, the pool has gone green, the villa needs a deep clean before guests arrive. Wants a real company that answers today, not a freelancer from a Telegram group.

Secondary: rental managers and agents who need a dependable subcontractor, and Thai/Chinese-speaking owners.

## Brand
Established visual identity, already in use across the fleet, uniforms, membership cards and ad creative. It is not up for reinvention.

- **Navy `#1B1B2F`** — the primary. Uniforms, vehicle wrap, packaging, logo.
- **Terracotta `#E27B54`** — the accent. Vehicle wrap, membership card, CTA blocks in the ad creative.
- The logomark is a four-point sparkle inside a squircle, wordmark `PHUKET ELITE CARE` above `PROPERTY MAINTENANCE`.
- The company's own positioning line: *"Premium service for luxury real estate."* Hotel-standard protocols applied to private villas.

## Tone
Concrete and unfussy. The company's real differentiator is *legitimacy*, not adjectives: it is a registered Thai company with its own salaried engineers, signed quotes, tax invoices and an office you can walk into. The copy should sound like a competent operations manager, not a luxury brochure. Name the thing that goes wrong and say who fixes it.

Claims that are true and may be used:
- 5.0★ from 23 reviews on Google (link to the listing; do not invent quote text).
- Licensed and insured; 10+ years on the island; 24/7 availability.
- Team handles English, Russian, Thai and Chinese.
- Office at 248/6 Moo 3 Srisoonthorn Road, Cherngtalay, Thalang, Phuket 83110.
- WhatsApp / phone +66 66 074 8105.
- Real rate card: Fresh clean ฿500 · General ฿1,100 · Deep ฿3,500 · Move-out ฿1,500 · Glass ฿3,000 · Deep scrubbing ฿7,500 · Villa Technical Check-up ฿5,900. Prices exclude 7% VAT and are confirmed after a free on-site assessment.
- Nine services: cleaning, pool, garden, AC, plumbing, electrical, renovation, rental & property management, villa technical check-up.

## Anti-references
- **Villa-rental listing sites** (Elite Havens and similar). This is a service company, not an inventory of properties. No property cards, no "from $X / night."
- **Cleaning-marketplace apps** (Helpling, Handy). No instant-booking widget, no per-hour pricing calculator, no "book a cleaner in 60 seconds."
- **Luxury-resort brochures.** No slow full-bleed carousels of empty infinity pools with a serif slogan floating over them. This company's photography shows people working.
- **The generic Phuket-agency template**: teal-and-white, palm-frond dividers, stock photography of a smiling model in a bikini beside a pool.
- The company's own current site is Tilda-generic; the point of this build is to beat it, not match it.

## Strategic principles
1. **The photography is documentary, not staged.** Every image of *work* is a real Elite Care employee on a real job. This is the single strongest trust signal available and it is what the anti-references cannot copy.

   **One deliberate exception, decided by the owner on 2026-07-10:** the hero background is a licensed stock photograph of a luxury villa (Pexels, Vero Benedini, free for commercial use), used because no photograph of a premium villa the company actually services exists yet. It is decorative and makes no claim of authorship. Replace it the moment the client supplies a real villa photo.

   **The exception does not extend to before/after renovation photos.** A stock or generated before/after would be a direct false claim about work performed. Those must be the company's own images or they do not ship; until then the renovation section offers to send the portfolio on request.
2. **Legitimacy over luxury adjectives.** "Signed quotes, tax invoices" outperforms "bespoke white-glove service." When choosing between an elegant phrase and a verifiable fact, take the fact.
3. **Never invent social proof.** The 5.0★/23 rating is real; the review *texts* were not obtainable. Link to the Google listing rather than write plausible-sounding testimonials.
4. **One conversion, everywhere.** Every CTA leads to the same WhatsApp thread. No competing secondary conversions (newsletter, callback form, chatbot).
5. **The quote form mirrors the office's real intake questions**, so the message that lands in WhatsApp is immediately actionable and no follow-up round-trip is needed.
6. **Multilingual reality.** English is the primary surface, but the site must state plainly that the team answers in RU/TH/CN, because that is a real purchase driver for this audience.
